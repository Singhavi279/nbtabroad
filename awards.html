What It's About

The Times Study Abroad Awards are designed to recognise what actually matters in global education – credible outcomes, informed choices, and institutions that deliver on their promise. At a time when more Indian students are investing significant time and money into studying abroad, these awards aim to cut through marketing noise and highlight real performance across the ecosystem.

The focus is on recognising institutions, organisations, and enablers that consistently deliver strong outcomes and uphold high standards across the study abroad ecosystem. Every recognition is backed by a structured evaluation process that looks beyond claims and factoring in outcomes, consistency, and impact.

By spotlighting genuine success stories and responsible practices, the initiative aims to build greater trust for students and families navigating this journey. Overall, the Times Study Abroad Awards align with a larger goal of supporting Indian students in making better decisions and strengthening India's position as a globally competitive talent base for the future.

FAQs

Can I apply in more than one category?
Yes. Applicants may apply in multiple relevant categories. However, a separate application form must be submitted for each category.
In which language should the application be submitted?
All applications must be submitted in English.
How do I submit my nomination?
Applicants must complete and submit the online application form available on the official awards website. All mandatory fields must be filled in before submission.
Is there an application fee?
Yes. The standard nomination fee is 40000 INR + GST, with an early bird fee of 35000 INR + GST for entries submitted within the early bird window.
Is it mandatory to submit supporting documents?
Certain supporting documents may be mandatory to verify eligibility and claims. Applicants are encouraged to provide relevant supporting information to strengthen their submission. Incomplete or unverifiable applications may not be considered for jury evaluation.
What happens after submission?
  • Applications will first be reviewed for eligibility and completeness.
  • Eligible entries will proceed to jury evaluation.
  • Shortlisted nominees may be contacted for clarification, if required.
  • Incomplete or ineligible applications will not proceed to the jury round.
Can I edit my application after submission?
Once the application is submitted, changes cannot be made. Applicants are advised to review all information carefully before final submission.
What is the last date for submission?
20 June 2026
When will the results be announced?
Winners will be announced at the official award ceremony. The date and venue are communicated on the website and through official communication channels.
Can an award be withheld?
The organisers reserve the right to withhold an award in any category if entries do not meet the required standards.
Who cannot participate?
Employees of the Awards Management, jury members, sponsors, and their immediate family members are not eligible to participate. Applications not meeting the eligibility criteria may be disqualified.
Where can I access the Terms & Conditions?
The detailed Terms & Conditions are available on the official website.
